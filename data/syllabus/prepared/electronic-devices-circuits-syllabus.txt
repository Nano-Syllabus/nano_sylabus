CURRICULUM - BE DEGREE IN ELECTRONICS AND COMMUNICATION ENGINEERING

Course: Electronic Devices and Circuits (EX 501)
Year: II
Part: I
Lecture: 3
Tutorial: 1
Practical: 3/2

Course objective:
To introduce fundamentals of electronic circuit analysis and provide basic understanding of semiconductor devices and analog integrated circuits.

Syllabus units:

1) Diodes (5 hours)
- The ideal diode
- Terminal characteristics of junction diodes
- Physical operation of diodes
- Analysis of diode circuits
- Small-signal model and applications
- Operation in reverse breakdown region (Zener diodes)

2) Bipolar Junction Transistor (10 hours)
- Operation of npn transistor in active mode
- Graphical representation of transistor characteristics
- DC analysis of transistor circuits
- Transistor as amplifier
- Small-signal equivalent circuit models
- Graphical load line analysis
- Biasing BJT for discrete-circuit design
- Basic single-stage BJT amplifier configurations (C-B, C-E, C-C)
- Transistor as switch (cutoff and saturation)
- Ebers-Moll large-signal BJT model

3) Field-Effect Transistor (9 hours)
- Structure and physical operation of enhancement-type MOSFET
- Current-voltage characteristics of enhancement-type MOSFET
- Depletion-type MOSFET
- MOSFET circuits at DC
- MOSFET as amplifier
- Biasing in MOS amplifier circuits
- Junction field-effect transistor (JFET)

4) Output Stages and Power Amplifiers (9 hours)
- Classification of output stages
- Class A output stage
- Class B output stage
- Class AB output stage
- Biasing class AB stage
- Power BJTs
- Transformer-coupled push-pull stages
- Tuned amplifiers

5) Signal Generator and Waveform-Shaping Circuits (6 hours)
- Basic principles of sinusoidal oscillator
- Op-amp RC oscillator circuits
- LC and crystal oscillators
- Square and triangular waveform generation using astable multivibrators
- Integrated circuit timers
- Precision rectifier circuits

6) Power Supplies, Breakdown Diodes, and Voltage Regulators (6 hours)
- Unregulated power supply
- Bandgap voltage reference and constant current diodes
- Transistor series regulators
- Improving regulator performance
- Current limiting
- Integrated circuit voltage regulator

Practical:
1. Bipolar junction transistor characteristics and single-stage amplifier
2. Field-effect transistor characteristics and single-stage amplifier
3. Power amplifiers
4. Relaxation oscillator and sinusoidal oscillator
5. Series and shunt voltage regulators

References:
1. A. S. Sedra and K. C. Smith, Microelectronic Circuits, Oxford University Press.
2. David A. Bell, Electronics Device and Circuits, PHI.
3. Robert Boylestad and Louis Nashelsky, Electronic Device and Circuit Theory, PHI.
4. Thomas L. Floyd, Electronic Devices, Pearson Education.
5. Mark N. Horenstein, Microelectronic Circuits and Devices, PHI.
6. Paul Horowitz and Winfield Hill, The Art of Electronics, Cambridge.
7. Jacob Millman, Christos C. Halkias, and Satyabrata Jit, Millman's Electronic Devices and Circuits, Tata McGraw-Hill.
