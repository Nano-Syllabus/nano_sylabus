CURRICULUM - BE DEGREE IN ELECTRONICS AND COMMUNICATION ENGINEERING

Course: ENGINEERING PHYSICS (SH 402)
Year: I
Part: I
Lecture: 4
Tutorial: 1
Practical: 2

Course objective:
To provide the concept and knowledge of physics with emphasis on present day applications.

Syllabus units:

1) Oscillation (7 hours)
- Mechanical oscillation: introduction
- Free oscillation
- Damped oscillation
- Forced mechanical oscillation
- EM oscillation: free, damped, and forced electromagnetic oscillation

2) Wave motion (2 hours)
- Waves and particles
- Progressive wave
- Energy, power, and intensity of progressive wave

3) Acoustics (3 hours)
- Reverberation
- Sabine's law
- Ultrasound and its applications

4) Physical optics (12 hours)
- Interference
  - Intensity in double slit interference
  - Interference in thin films
  - Newton's rings
  - Haidinger fringes
- Diffraction
  - Fresnel and Fraunhofer diffraction
  - Intensity due to a single slit
  - Diffraction grating
  - X-ray diffraction and x-ray for material test
- Polarization
  - Double refraction
  - Nicol prism and wave plates
  - Optical activity and specific rotation

5) Geometrical optics (3 hours)
- Lenses and combination of lenses
- Cardinal points
- Chromatic aberration

6) Laser and fiber optics (4 hours)
- Laser production
  - He-Ne laser
  - Uses of laser
- Fiber optics
  - Self focusing
  - Applications of optical fiber

7) Electrostatics (8 hours)
- Electric charge and force
- Electric field and potential
- Electrostatic potential energy
- Capacitors and capacitor with dielectric
- Charging and discharging of a capacitor

8) Electromagnetism (11 hours)
- Direct current
  - Electric current
  - Ohm's law, resistance and resistivity
  - Semiconductor and superconductor
- Magnetic fields
  - Magnetic force and torque
  - Hall effect
  - Cyclotron and synchrotron
  - Biot-Savart law
  - Ampere's circuit law, magnetic fields around straight conductors
  - Faraday's laws, induction and energy transformation, induced field
  - LR circuit and induced magnetic field
  - Displacement current

9) Electromagnetic waves (5 hours)
- Maxwell's equations
- Wave equations and speed
- E and B fields
- Continuity equation
- Energy transfer

10) Photon and matter waves (5 hours)
- Quantization of energy
- Electrons and matter waves
- Schrodinger wave equation
- Probability distribution
- One dimensional potential well
- Uncertainty principle
- Barrier tunneling

Total theory hours: 60

Practical list:
1. Determine acceleration due to gravity and radius of gyration of a bar about an axis through its center of gravity.
2. Determine modulus of elasticity of given materials and moment of inertia of a circular disc using torsion pendulum.
3. Determine angle of prism and dispersive power of prism materials using spectrometer.
4. Determine wavelength of sodium light by Newton's rings.
5. Determine wavelength of He-Ne laser and use it to measure thickness of a thin wire by diffraction of light.
6. Study variation of angle of rotation of plane of polarization using concentration of cane sugar solution.
7. Determine specific rotation of cane sugar solution using polarimeter.
8. Determine low resistance of a wire by Carey Foster bridge and resistance per unit length.
9. Determine capacitance of a capacitor by charging and discharging through resistor.
10. Plot graph between current and frequency in an LRC series circuit and find resonant frequency and quality factor.
11. Determine dielectric constant of a substance and study variation with frequency by resonance method.
12. Determine susceptibility of a solution by Quinke's method.
13. Study electric field mapping.
