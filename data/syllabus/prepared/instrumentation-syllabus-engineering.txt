CURRICULUM - BE DEGREE IN ELECTRONICS AND COMMUNICATION ENGINEERING

Course: Instrumentation
Year: II
Part: I
Lecture: 4
Tutorial: 1
Practical: 3

Course objective:
To provide the basic concept of instrumentation and knowledge of microprocessor-based instrumentation systems for interfacing peripherals, circuit design techniques, and instruments for a wide range of measurement problems.

Syllabus units:

1) Instrumentation System (2 hours)
- Analog and digital signals and systems
- Instrumentation system: definition, block diagram, component descriptions
- Need of electrical, electronic, pneumatic, and hydraulic working media systems and conversion devices

2) Theory of Measurement (6 hours)
- Static performance parameters: accuracy, precision, sensitivity, resolution, linearity
- Dynamic performance parameters: frequency response, bandwidth, response time
- Errors in measurement and statistical analysis
- Measurement of voltage and current (moving coil and moving iron instruments)
- Measurement of resistance (low, medium, and high)
- AC bridges: Wheatstone bridge, Maxwell's bridge, Schering bridge

3) Transducer (8 hours)
- Introduction and classification
- Applications of transducers:
  - Mechanical variables: displacement, strain, velocity, acceleration, vibration
  - Process variables: temperature, pressure, level, fluid
  - Bio-physical variables: blood pressure, myoelectric potential

4) Microprocessor Base System (2 hours)
- Basic features, advantages, and disadvantages of microprocessor-based instrumentation systems
- Types: open-loop and closed-loop
- PC interfacing techniques
- Review of address decoding, mapping I/O, and interfacing of I/O ports and memories

5) Parallel Interfacing with Microprocessor Based System (6 hours)
- Methods of parallel data transfer: simple I/O, simple strobe I/O, single and double handshake I/O
- 8255 as a general-purpose programmable peripheral device: block diagram, modes of operation, control words
- Interfacing examples: I/O ports, keyboard, printer, seven-segment display
- Introduction to ISA and PCI bus

6) Serial Interfacing with Microprocessor Based System (6 hours)
- Advantages and disadvantages of serial transfer over parallel
- Basic concepts in serial I/O:
  - Interfacing requirements
  - Alphanumeric codes
  - Transmission formats: synchronous/asynchronous, simplex/duplex, transmission rate
  - Error and error checks in data communication
  - Data communication over telephone lines
  - Standards: RS-232, RS-422, RS-423
  - Interfacing examples
- USB: standards, signals, throughput, protocols, wireless USB, and on-the-go

7) Interfacing ADC and DAC (5 hours)
- DAC: characteristics, weighted resistor type, R-2R ladder type
- ADC: characteristics, successive approximation, ramp, dual-slope, flash
- Interfacing ADC and DAC to microprocessor
- Errors in ADC and DAC

8) Data Acquisition System (2 hours)
- Data acquisition system
- Data loggers
- Data archiving and storage
- Bluetooth devices and characteristics

9) Grounding and Shielding (4 hours)
- Outline for grounding, shielding, and safety
- Noise: sources, coupling mechanisms, prevention
- Grounding: single-point grounding, ground plane, ground loop
- Filtering: bandwidth minimization, ferrite beads, decoupling capacitors, line filters, isolators, transient suppressors
- Shielding: definition and types
- Protection against electrostatic discharge
- General design rules

10) Circuit Design (3 hours)
- Converting requirements into design
- Reliability and fault tolerance
- High-speed design: bandwidth, decoupling, ground bounce, crosstalk, impedance matching, timing
- Low-power design
- Reset and power-failure detection

11) Circuit Layout (3 hours)
- Circuit boards and PCBs
- Component placement
- Routing signal traces: trace density, common impedance, signal/return distribution, transmission line concerns, impedance matching, crosstalk avoidance
- Grounds, returns, and shields
- Connectors and cables
- Testing and maintenance

12) Software for Instrumentation System (3 hours)
- Types of software, selection, and purchase
- Software models with metrics, advantages, and limitations
- Risk abatement and failure prevention: development plan, safety, reliability, fault tolerance
- Software bugs and testing
- Good programming practice

13) Electrical Equipments (4 hours)
- Wattmeter: types and working principle
- Energy meter: types and working principle
- Frequency meter: types and working principle
- Power factor meter: types and working principle

14) Case Study (6 hours)
- Local industrial case studies with attention to:
  - measurement requirements and accuracy
  - hardware employed
  - environmental operating conditions
  - signal processing and transmission
  - output devices
- Example areas:
  - instrumentation for power stations (electrical and non-electrical parameters)
  - instrumentation for wire and cable manufacturing and bottling plants
  - instrumentation for beverage manufacturing and bottling plants
  - instrumentation for biomedical applications (clinic/hospital)
  - other industries as approved by subject teacher

Total theory hours: 60

Practical:
1. Use of resistive, inductive, and other types of transducers.
2. Review of assembly programming and simple I/O interfacing with 8255.
3. Interfacing of LEDs and seven-segment display.
4. Interfacing of motors.
5. Interfacing of different types of ADC.
6. Interfacing of different types of DAC.
7. Small group project: design of a simple microprocessor-based instrumentation system.

References:

2. Ramesh S. Gaonkar, Microprocessor Architecture, Programming and Application with 8085, Prentice Hall.
3. K. R. Fowler, Electronic Instrument Design: Architecting for the Life Cycle, Oxford University Press.
4. Jan Axelson, USB Complete, Penram International Publishing.
5. A. K. Sawhney, A Course in Electronic Measurement and Instrumentation, Dhanpat Rai and Sons.
6. J. B. Gupta, A Course in Electrical and Electronics Measurement and Instrumentation, Kataria and Sons.
